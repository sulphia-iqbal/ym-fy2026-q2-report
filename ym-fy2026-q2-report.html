Letter from the Finance Team

We begin in the name of Allah ﷻ and send peace and blessings upon His Messenger ﷺ

What a quarter it has been. Between YMC bringing our community together in Baltimore, chapters across the country serving their communities through YM Gives and other amazing events, our sisters showing up in D.C. for Muslim Advocacy Day, and the exciting groundwork laid ahead for YMLC this summer — Q2 was full of khair. This report is the story of that khair, told in numbers.

This quarter also took us on the road. National Shura tours to Georgia and Dallas let us sit face-to-face with local leadership — hearing what's working, what's been confusing, and what chapters actually need from us. Those conversations directly shaped the systems and reminders you'll find in these pages.

So why do we care this much about receipts and spreadsheets and all of this seemingly boring, mundane stuff? A man once asked the Prophet ﷺ whether he should tie his camel or trust in Allah ﷻ — and the Prophet ﷺ told him to tie it and trust in Allah ﷻ. That's exactly what this work is. We make du'a for barakah in YM's efforts, and then we tie our camel: the JotForms, the dashboards, the receipts. All the admin work behind the tarbiyyah and spiritual development happening in our organization — that's us tying our camel.

إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَأْمُرُكُمْ أَنْ تُؤَدُّوا الْأَمَانَاتِ إِلَىٰ أَهْلِهَا

"Indeed, Allah commands you to render trusts to whom they are due."

Qur'an 4:58

Every dollar YM holds is exactly that — a trust from the community. So when you submit a JotForm to track your finances, upload a proper receipt, or keep your dashboard current — that's not paperwork, that's amanah. Because of your cooperation and attention to detail, we can account for every dollar YM earned or spent this quarter, and hand the next group of leaders records they can actually plan from. We're not just protecting money; we're protecting trust, barakah, and the reputation of this work.

We ask Allah ﷻ to accept our efforts, place barakah in every dollar entrusted to this organization, and keep our chapters growing in strength, sisterhood, and service.

With much love,

YM Sisters National Finance Team

Notes from Finance

Handbook launch & friendly policy reminders
The NN Finance Handbook is here! 🎉 We're excited to announce the release of the NN Finance Handbook — your complete guide to accounts, JotForm submission types, dashboards, approvals, lump sums, audits, and a full worked case study. Read it here →
Friendly Financial Policy Reminders
1

Zelle is now available for reimbursements Update

Prefer Zelle over Venmo when possible. Coordinate Zelle details with finance@ymsisters.com after submitting the JotForm.

2

No personal expenses on the same receipt as YM expenses Strict

Mixed receipts will be denied — we are strict on this. Make YM purchases on their own receipt.

3

Proper event names in Expense Details are required

Format: Date · Event Name · Subcategory (e.g., 1/9/26 NJ Gala – Decorations). Some NNs are omitting event names and being contacted individually — save everyone the back-and-forth by labeling correctly the first time.

4

NN collab confusion, cleared up

When two NNs split costs, up to 3 JotForms are needed — (①) the payer's reimbursement expense, (②) the other NN's expense for sending their share, (③) the receiving NN's income. See Handbook §05b for the full worked example.

5

Submit separate JotForms for each event

We don't want multiple events' expenses combined into one JotForm. If you paid for or attended more than one event, submit a separate JotForm for each event so we can track and approve them individually.

6

Also worth remembering

  • Receipt format: PDFs, not screenshots, for online orders.
  • As always — no receipt, no approval.
